01 — Philosophy

An evolving
tradition.

Two kanji sit at the heart of every class. They are the values we pass on — to a five-year-old's first bow, to a parent training next to their teenager, to the senior black belt teaching the next generation.
伝統
Dentō

Tradition

The forms, the etiquette, the lineage of teachers behind every technique we drill. We honor the path our art was carried down, because the discipline of those who came before is what gives our training its weight.

進化
Shinka

Evolution

Each generation of student tests, refines, and presses the art forward. We teach what works — so that what your child learns on the mat shows up off it: at school, at home, in the way they carry themselves for life.

“The dojo is a small place to practice becoming the person you intend to be everywhere else.” — Sensei's reminder, first night of every term
02 — For Families

A safe place to grow up.

Many of our students arrive straight from school and stay until dinnertime. Parents read on the bench. Friendships form between drills. The art is serious — the atmosphere is warm. It's the kind of place you'd want for your own kid, because it's the kind of place we built for ours.
安心
Anshin

Safe & supervised

An open viewing area so parents can watch every class. Small class sizes. Instructors who know each child by name, watch their progress, and notice the day they need a little extra encouragement.

家族
Kazoku

A second family

Two and three generations of Mandeville families have trained on this floor. Train alongside your child in our weekly family class — or sit on the bench, sip your coffee, and watch them light up.

仲間
Nakama

Friendships that stick

Many of our students grow up together — birthdays on the mat, summer training, lifelong friendships forged in shared effort. Shy kids find their voice. Quiet kids find their people.

成長
Seichō

What you'll see at home

Better focus at school. More patience with siblings. A child who looks adults in the eye, finishes what they started, and bows before they walk through the door. Parents tell us they notice within weeks.

04 — The Way

The four pillars.

Karate isn't just punching and kicking. Every belt promotion is earned against the same four pillars — and they're the same four traits parents tell us they see show up at home, at school, in the way their child carries themselves.
礼
Rei

Respect

For the dojo, for one's training partners, and for the lineage. Karate begins and ends with a bow — it is the doorway through which all training passes.

忍
Nin

Perseverance

Showing up on the days you don't feel like it. The willingness to be a beginner in front of others — and the patience to do the rep again, slower, better.

克
Koku

Self-Control

The art of choosing your response. Power is meaningful only when paired with the discipline to decide when, how, and whether to use it.

勇
Yū

Spirit

Indomitable spirit — the will to keep going. We train the body to teach the mind a habit that serves the student long after class ends.
