Meet Your Instructor

Sensei Scott

Founder · Head Instructor · Navy Veteran · Dad

Sensei Scott began his martial arts journey in 1995 at Mandeville Karate, earning his black belt in 2001 as part of three National Champion teams. From 2003 to 2008 he served in the U.S. Navy — much of it in Guam with the Navy's top helicopter squadron — exploring a range of martial arts systems that broadened his perspective on combat and practical self-protection.

He returned home in 2015 to take over Mandeville Karate, and in 2020 became a father — a milestone that deepened his purpose. That same year he began developing Jissen-Ryu with a clear mission: to make karate real again. Not a performance art, but a practical protection system he'd be proud to teach his own children. In 2026, that vision became Jissen Martial Arts.

Our Story

From Mandeville Karate to Jissen

Jissen Martial Arts began as Mandeville Karate Training Center, established in 1987. Sensei Scott started training there in 1995 and earned his black belt in 2001. After taking over the school in 2015, he began shaping what would become Jissen-Ryu — a personal system built around practical application and realistic training.

"Less memorization, more learning. Less rigid structure, more live drilling."

Instead of standing in place punching and kicking the air, students train in well-rounded striking, clinching, takedowns, self-defense, and situational awareness. To better reflect that system and vision, the school was reimagined as Jissen Martial Arts — a smaller, more personal facility with outdoor training space.
