our story

A dojo with
roots in this town.

Jissen-Ryu opened its doors on Highway 22 in 1987. The bench out front has held three generations of Mandeville parents — grandparents who once trained here, parents reading while their kids spar, kids waiting for their turn to bow in.

The art we teach has roots older than this dojo. The way we teach it has been shaped, gently, by every family that's ever called this place a second home.

— Sensei Smith, since day one

the four virtues

What every belt is measured by.

Karate is not just punching and kicking. Every belt your child earns is built on these four — the principles that shape a student into a martial artist.

礼
Rei

Respect

The bow that begins and ends every class — and the habit it builds outside the dojo.

忍
Nin

Perseverance

Showing up on the days you don't feel like it. Doing the rep again, slower, better.

克
Koku

Self-Control

The art of choosing your response. Power paired with the discipline to choose when.

勇
Yū

Spirit

The will to keep going. The quiet kind of brave kids carry with them for life.
